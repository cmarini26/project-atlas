# Project Atlas — Founder's Bible

## Vision

Build an AI marketing employee for small businesses.

Project Atlas learns a business, builds a Digital Twin of it, identifies marketing opportunities, creates campaigns, prepares content, and helps execute marketing consistently — without being asked.

## Working Product Name

MarketingOS

## What Atlas Is

Atlas is not a tool. It is an AI employee.

A great marketing employee does not wait to be prompted. They know the business, watch for opportunities, prepare work in advance, and bring recommendations to the decision-maker for approval.

Atlas does exactly that — continuously, at scale, for every business it serves.

## Core Concepts

### Digital Twin

Every company in Atlas has a Digital Twin — a living, structured model of the business that Atlas builds and maintains over time.

The Digital Twin captures:

- Catalog and inventory
- Brand identity and voice
- Audience and customer segments
- Competitive positioning
- Marketing history and performance
- Seasonal and temporal context

The Digital Twin is the foundation of everything Atlas does. Without it, the system is just a content generator. With it, Atlas becomes a genuine marketing intelligence system.

### Business Brain

The Business Brain is the knowledge layer of the Digital Twin.

It stores structured Facts, derived Knowledge, and Observations made over time. It is what allows Atlas to answer questions like:

> What does this business sell?
> Who is their customer?
> What worked last time?
> What has changed since last week?

The Business Brain grows smarter over time. Atlas should know more about the business tomorrow than it knows today.

### Opportunity Engine

The Opportunity Engine continuously scans the Business Brain for marketing opportunities — moments where a campaign would be timely, relevant, and likely to perform.

Opportunities are scored by:

- Relevance (does this match what the business cares about?)
- Timing (is now the right moment?)
- Confidence (how certain are we this will work?)
- Urgency (will this opportunity expire?)

### Decision Engine

The Decision Engine takes scored Opportunities and decides which to act on.

Every Decision must explain itself. It must answer:

- Why now?
- Why this?
- Why this channel?
- Why do we expect this to work?

Decisions produce Recommendations. Recommendations produce Campaigns. Campaigns require human Approval before execution.

## The Atlas Loop

Observe → Understand → Decide → Recommend → Prepare → Approve → Execute → Measure → Learn

This loop is continuous. It does not stop after the first campaign. Every execution produces Learning that feeds back into the Business Brain and improves future Decisions.

## Initial Validation Markets

### Dynamic Inventory Businesses

The first validation market is any business with a dynamic product catalog that changes frequently and benefits from consistent marketing of individual items.

This includes:

- Comic book auction houses (primary design partner: CBB Auctions)
- Exotic used car dealerships (second validation vertical)

These businesses share a common pattern: high-value, visually interesting inventory that turns over regularly and benefits from campaign-level attention for individual items.

### CBB Auctions — Primary Design Partner

CBB Auctions is a comic book auction house and marketplace. They run periodic auctions, manage seller stores and inventory, and deal in high-value collectibles.

Atlas for CBB will:

- Monitor active auctions and ending-soon items
- Identify high-value or featured listings worth promoting
- Recommend auction promotion campaigns
- Generate social, email, and other content for approval
- Learn what drove engagement and bids over time

CBB is the primary design partner for the Atlas platform. Their feedback will shape core platform decisions.

### Exotic Dealerships — Second Validation Vertical

Exotic used car dealerships have dynamic inventory of high-value, visually compelling vehicles. A new vehicle arriving or a price drop is a natural marketing moment.

Atlas for dealerships will:

- Crawl inventory weekly
- Select a featured vehicle
- Generate a week of social, email, and ad content
- Surface it for owner approval

## Product Principle

The user should not need to think like a marketer.

The system should answer:

> What would a great marketing employee do next?

## MVP

The MVP proves the core loop for a single business:

Website URL or data feed
→ Catalog extraction
→ Business Brain populated
→ Opportunity identified
→ Decision made
→ Campaign recommendation surfaced
→ Content prepared (social, email)
→ Human approval
→ Ready to publish

## Differentiation

Most tools help users create marketing.

Project Atlas decides what marketing should be created next — and prepares it before being asked.
