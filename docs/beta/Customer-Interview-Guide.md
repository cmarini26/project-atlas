# Customer Interview Guide

**Purpose:** a structured set of questions for real conversations with Stage A private beta customers (5–10 hand-picked businesses, per [Version-1.0-Roadmap.md](../plans/Version-1.0-Roadmap.md)). This guide exists because Stage A's whole job is learning whether the core loop — observe, recommend, explain, approve — actually works for real businesses, not scaling. The questions below are how that learning gets captured on purpose instead of accumulating as founder gut feeling.

**How to use this guide:**
- These are conversations, not surveys. Ask the questions in your own words, follow up on anything interesting, and don't rush through a checklist in front of the customer.
- Record what you learn in [Founder-Learning-Log.md](Founder-Learning-Log.md) within 24 hours, while it's fresh. A memory of "they seemed happy" a week later is not data.
- The four sections below (onboarding, first recommendation, week one, month one) mirror the natural checkpoints in a customer's actual lifecycle with the product — not a fixed calendar, so if a customer reaches "first recommendation" on day one, ask that section's questions on day one.
- The open-ended section has no fixed timing — use it whenever a conversation opens up beyond the structured checkpoints.
- Note which persona a customer resembles (Marcus — owner-operator, low time, moderate technical comfort; Sofia — marketing contractor managing multiple client businesses; see [Landing-Page.md](../marketing/Landing-Page.md) §1) — the two personas will likely answer some of these questions differently, and that difference is itself useful signal.
- Be honest in the conversation about what is and isn't real yet (see [Private-Beta-Execution.md](../plans/Private-Beta-Execution.md) §2, "Publishing Expectations") — this guide is a tool for learning the truth from customers, not for managing their perception of the product.

---

## 1. Questions to Ask During Onboarding

Ideally asked while watching the customer onboard (screen share or in person) — onboarding takes minutes, so this is a short, live conversation, not a follow-up call.

- Before they start: what do you think Atlas is going to do for you? What's the one thing you're hoping happens?
- While entering company name and industry: does anything about this step feel like it's asking for information Atlas should already know, or shouldn't need to ask?
- While entering the website URL: any hesitation before submitting it? Any concern about what Atlas will do with it? (See [Trust & Security](../marketing/Landing-Page.md) — this is the moment to hear, unprompted, whether that copy actually addresses real concerns.)
- On the "where do your customers find you" marketing-presence step: do the channel options match how you actually think about your own marketing presence? If you skipped it, why?
- After submitting: what do you expect to happen next, and how long do you expect to wait?

---

## 2. Questions After First Recommendation

Ideally asked while reading the first recommendation together, so you can watch their reaction to each part of it, not just their summary afterward.

- Read the recommendation as a whole: does this sound like it's about *your* business, or does it feel like it could be about any business in your industry?
- Read each of the four rationale quadrants (why now / why this / why this channel / why it will work) one at a time: does this reason make sense to you? Would you have thought of this yourself?
- What's your gut reaction to the confidence score? Does that number mean anything to you, or is it just a number on the screen?
- Before they act on it: what are you going to do — approve, edit, or pass? Why?
- If they **Approve**: what do you think happens the moment you click it? *(Listen carefully for whether they believe the content is about to reach a real audience. If so, and they haven't already been told publishing is currently simulated — see [Private-Beta-Execution.md](../plans/Private-Beta-Execution.md) §2 "Publishing Expectations" — that is a critical finding to log and correct immediately, not a detail to gloss over.)*
- If they choose **Edit & Approve**: what did you change, and why? Was it about tone, a fact, the channel, or something else?
- If they choose **Not This Time**: what specifically was wrong — timing, channel, content, or something else? Did the optional note field feel useful, or like a chore?
- How long did the whole thing take, in their own estimate, start to finish? (Compare this against the actual logged elapsed time — see [Beta-Success-Metrics.md](Beta-Success-Metrics.md) §2.)

## 3. Questions After One Week

- Have you looked at Atlas again since the first recommendation? Roughly how many times?
- Has a new recommendation appeared? What did you think of it compared to the first one?
- Is there anything you expected Atlas to do by now that it hasn't?
- Have you told anyone else about Atlas yet — a partner, an employee, another business owner? What did you tell them?
- Has anything about the product confused you, or made you double back to figure out what happened?
- Honestly — would you have kept opening it this week if nobody from our side had checked in with you?

---

## 4. Questions After One Month

- Thinking back to week one versus now: does Atlas feel like it knows your business better? Give me a specific example if you can.
- Has your approval behavior changed — are you approving more, editing less, rejecting differently than you were in week one?
- What's the single most useful thing Atlas has told you or shown you this month?
- What's the single most annoying or confusing thing?
- If Atlas disappeared tomorrow, what would you miss? What would you genuinely not miss?
- Would you pay for this? If yes, roughly what would feel fair? If no, what would have to change?
- Has there been a moment where you wished Atlas could actually publish somewhere real, instead of showing you a draft?

---

## 5. Open-Ended Discovery Questions

Use any time, not tied to a specific checkpoint — these are for the moments a conversation opens up beyond the structured schedule above.

- Walk me through how you used to handle marketing before Atlas. What did a typical week look like?
- If you could wave a magic wand and have Atlas do one more thing, what would it be?
- Was there a competitor or tool you compared Atlas to, even just mentally? What made you choose to try Atlas instead, or alongside it?
- What almost made you not sign up?
- Has anything about Atlas surprised you — good or bad?
- Is there anyone else in your business who should be part of these conversations?
- Was there a moment you didn't trust one of Atlas's recommendations? What was it, and what would have made you trust it?
